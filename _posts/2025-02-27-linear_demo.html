linearly separable Demo


Instructor

Original transformation formula:

\[ x'= \begin{bmatrix} (x_1 - \bar{x}_1)^2 \\ (x_2 - \bar{x}_2)^2 \end{bmatrix} = (x - \bar{x})^{\top} (x - \bar{x}) \]

Step 1. Randomly generating some points, we find that it is linearly indistinguishable, so the transformation is projected using the above equation.

Step 2. We choose a point \( (\bar{x}_1,\bar{x}_2) \) at random and assume that \( \bar{x}_1 = 0,\bar{x}_2 = 0\).

Step 3. Click on the Transform button to see the transformation animation utilizing the formulas.

Examples. I offer an example for reference

\[ ( 1.5 , 2 )-> \begin{bmatrix} (1.5 - 0)^2 \\ (2 - 0)^2 \end{bmatrix} -> \begin{bmatrix} 2.25 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \]

Therefore, the original point \( (1.5, 2) \) becomes \( (2.25, 4) \) after projection.

Conclusion. All points go from linearly indivisible to linearly divisible after projection.
